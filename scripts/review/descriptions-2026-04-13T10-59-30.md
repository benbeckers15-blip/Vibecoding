**¶2 — The Wines:**
The 15-acre estate produces between 1,800 and 2,000 dozen bottles a year, depending on the season — all estate-grown and vinified on site. Cabernet Sauvignon is the flagship: the inaugural 2000 vintage took Best Wine at the 2001 Sheraton Wine Awards, a result that established the winery's reputation early. Reds are hand-picked, sorted, fermented in small open-top vessels, hand-plunged daily for up to ten days, basket pressed, and aged in French barriques for 12 to 18 months. Whites are harvested in the cool early hours and fermented in a mix of new and used French oak. The approach is traditional and deliberate — minimal intervention, with the emphasis on what the site produces rather than what winemaking can add to it. Since 2020, winemaker Conrad Tritt has overseen both vineyard and cellar. He came to Margaret River in 2007 with a surfboard and stayed; his subsequent years at Pierro, Watershed, and Vasse Felix gave him a thorough grounding in what the region can do.

**¶3 — The Story:**
The Karri-Davies family connection to this part of Western Australia runs to 1875, when Robert's great-great-grandfather built a timber mill at Karridale, constructed the jetties at Hamelin and Flinders Bay, and completed the Cape Leeuwin Lighthouse. Robert and Karen planted the vineyard in 1996 on land that Dr. Tom Cullity had originally identified for Vasse Felix — the founding estate of the entire Margaret River wine industry. That the site eventually became Cape Grace rather than Vasse Felix is one of the region's more quietly interesting footnotes. Most of the wine is sold through the cellar door or direct to a mailing list, which means turning up in person remains the most reliable way to access it.

The land at Glenarty Road has been in Ben McDonald's family since his grandfather William — known as Aussie — cleared virgin jarrah and marri scrub under the Group Settlement Scheme in the 1940s.
